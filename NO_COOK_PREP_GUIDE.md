### Step-by-Step Instructions for Every Item in the No-Cook Meal Plan

## PART 1 — How to Prepare Each Item

---

### 1. Overnight Oats (ஓட்ஸ்)
**When to prepare:** Previous night at 9:00 PM
**Ready at:** Next morning 8:30 AM (8–10 hours later)

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| Step 1 | Take 1 cup rolled oats (ஓட்ஸ்) in a bowl or glass jar |
| Step 2 | Add 1 cup water OR 200ml low-fat milk (பால்) |
| Step 3 | Stir once. Cover with a plate or lid |
| Step 4 | Leave at room temperature overnight (no fridge needed) |
| Step 5 | Morning: Add banana (வாழைப்பழம்) + chia seeds (சியா விதை) + honey — stir and eat |

> **Savory version:** Add curd (தயிர்) instead of milk + grated cucumber (வெள்ளரி) + grated carrot (கேரட்) + pinch salt + lemon (எலுமிச்சை)
> **⚠️ Eat within 12 hours of soaking. Do NOT keep for next day.**

---

### 2. Chia Pudding (சியா விதை புட்டிங்)
**When to prepare:** Previous night at 9:00 PM
**Ready at:** Next morning 8:30 AM (8–10 hours later)

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| Step 1 | Take 2 tablespoons chia seeds (சியா விதை) in a glass or bowl |
| Step 2 | Add 200ml low-fat milk (பால்) or thin buttermilk (மோர்) |
| Step 3 | Stir well for 30 seconds — seeds will clump, keep stirring |
| Step 4 | Stir again after 5 minutes to break clumps |
| Step 5 | Cover and leave overnight at room temperature |
| Step 6 | Morning: Mixture should be thick like pudding. Add sliced banana (வாழைப்பழம்) + soaked almonds (பாதாம்) + honey |

> **⚠️ Chia pudding made with milk without fridge is safe for up to 10–12 hours only. Make it exactly at bedtime (9–9:30 PM) and eat by 8:30 AM.**

---

### 3. Soaked Poha (அவல்)
**When to prepare:** 10 minutes before eating (8:20 AM for 8:30 breakfast / 1:20 PM for 1:30 lunch)
**Ready in:** 10 minutes

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| Step 1 | Take 1.5 cups thick poha (அவல்) in a colander / sieve (வடிகட்டி) |
| Step 2 | Pour water gently over the poha — just enough to wet it |
| Step 3 | Let it sit for 8–10 minutes |
| Step 4 | Gently squeeze the poha — it should be soft but not mushy |
| Step 5 | Transfer to a plate. Add: chopped onion (வெங்காயம்) + chopped tomato (தக்காளி) + roasted groundnuts (வறுத்த நிலக்கடலை) + lemon juice (எலுமிச்சை சாறு) + salt + green chilli (பச்சை மிளகாய்) |
| Step 6 | Mix well and eat immediately |

> **For Lunch version:** Add sprouted moong (முளைகட்டிய பாசிப்பயறு) + curd (தயிர்) for extra protein
> **⚠️ Eat immediately after soaking. Do not leave soaked poha for later.**

---

### 4. Sprouted Moong Dal (முளைகட்டிய பாசிப்பயறு)
**When to start:** Sunday night and Wednesday night
**Ready in:** 36–48 hours (Tuesday morning and Friday morning)

| Step | Action | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Step 1 | Take 1 cup whole green moong dal (பாசிப் பருப்பு) | Sunday 9 PM |
| Step 2 | Wash 2–3 times in clean water | — |
| Step 3 | Soak in 3 cups of water, cover loosely | Sunday 9 PM |
| Step 4 | After 8 hours, drain all water completely | Monday 5–6 AM |
| Step 5 | Tie the drained moong in a wet muslin cloth / thin cotton cloth | Monday morning |
| Step 6 | Hang the cloth OR keep the bowl covered with a wet cloth in a dark corner | — |
| Step 7 | Rinse once by opening the cloth, pouring water over, draining — cover again | Monday night |
| Step 8 | Rinse again next morning. Small white tails (sprouts) appear | Tuesday morning |
| Step 9 | By Tuesday afternoon, sprouts are 1–2cm long — ready to eat raw | Tuesday lunch ✅ |

> **Storage (no fridge):** Place sprouts in a bowl covered with a plate. Rinse once daily. Use within 1.5–2 days.
> **In hot weather:** Use within 1 day of sprouting. If they smell sour, discard.
> **Wednesday night:** Repeat the same process for Friday/Saturday sprouts.

---

### 5. Sprouted Chana / Chickpea (முளைகட்டிய கொண்டைக்கடலை)
**When to start:** Sunday night and Wednesday night (alongside moong)
**Ready in:** 48–60 hours (longer than moong)

| Step | Action | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Step 1 | Take 1/2 cup dry chana (கொண்டைக்கடலை) | Sunday 9 PM |
| Step 2 | Wash thoroughly | — |
| Step 3 | Soak in 2 cups water, cover — chana absorbs a lot | Sunday 9 PM |
| Step 4 | After 12 hours, drain completely | Monday 9 AM |
| Step 5 | Tie in a wet cloth / keep in covered bowl | Monday morning |
| Step 6 | Rinse every 8–10 hours | Monday night + Tuesday morning |
| Step 7 | Sprouts appear by Tuesday evening | Tuesday evening |
| Step 8 | Sprouts are 1cm long and ready to eat by Wednesday morning | Wednesday ✅ |

> **Tip:** Chana sprouts take longer than moong. Start chana at Sunday night for Wednesday meals.
> **Eat raw in salad** — just add lemon (எலுமிச்சை) + salt + chaat masala + onion (வெங்காயம்)

---

### 6. Soaked Almonds (பாதாம் பருப்பு)
**When to prepare:** Previous night at 9:00 PM
**Ready at:** Next morning (8+ hours)

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| Step 1 | Take 10–15 almonds (பாதாம் பருப்பு) in a small bowl or cup |
| Step 2 | Pour just enough water to cover them fully |
| Step 3 | Leave at room temperature overnight |
| Step 4 | Morning: Drain water, gently peel the brown skin off each almond |
| Step 5 | Eat peeled almonds directly |

> **Why peel?** Skin contains tannins that block nutrient absorption. Peeled soaked almonds give 3x more calcium and protein benefit.
> **Can soak in bulk** — soak 30–40 almonds on Sunday for Mon–Wed. Keep in a dry plate, covered with cloth.

---

### 7. Hung Curd (தூக்கிய தயிர்)
**When to prepare:** Previous night at 9:00 PM
**Ready at:** Next morning (8–10 hours)

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| Step 1 | Take a thin cotton cloth (muslin / மெல்லிய துணி) or a clean dupatta piece |
| Step 2 | Pour 1–1.5 cups of fresh curd (தயிர்) into the centre of the cloth |
| Step 3 | Gather the corners, tie with a rubber band |
| Step 4 | Hang the cloth bag over a bowl (the watery whey drips out into the bowl) |
| Step 5 | Leave overnight |
| Step 6 | Morning: Open the cloth — thick creamy hung curd is ready |
| Step 7 | Add salt + lemon + cumin powder (சீரகப் பொடி) for savory dressing OR honey for sweet version |

> **Whey water (whey — மோர் நீர்) collected in the bowl:** Don't throw it — drink it or add to soaked oats. It's full of protein.
> **Yield:** 1 cup curd → approximately 1/2 cup hung curd

---

### 8. Sprouted Mixed Dal Salad (முளைகட்டிய கலவை சாலட்)
**When to assemble:** 5 minutes before lunch (1:25 PM)
**Uses:** Ready sprouts from your 2x weekly sprouting batch

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| Step 1 | Take 1 cup ready sprouts (moong + chana mix) |
| Step 2 | Rinse in clean water once, drain |
| Step 3 | Add: finely diced onion (வெங்காயம்) + diced tomato (தக்காளி) + diced cucumber (வெள்ளரி) |
| Step 4 | Squeeze 1/2 lemon (எலுமிச்சை) over it |
| Step 5 | Add salt + chaat masala + chopped green chilli (பச்சை மிளகாய்) if desired |
| Step 6 | Top with 1 bowl curd (தயிர்) OR drizzle hung curd dressing |
| Step 7 | Optional: Add grated carrot (கேரட்) or grated raw beetroot (செங்கிழங்கு) |

---

### 9. Muesli Fruit Bowl (மியூஸ்லி பழ கிண்ணம்)
**When to prepare:** 2 minutes before eating
**No soaking needed**

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| Step 1 | Take 1 cup store-bought oat-based muesli (மியூஸ்லி) — no added sugar preferred |
| Step 2 | Add 150ml low-fat curd (தயிர்) or buttermilk (மோர்) |
| Step 3 | Mix well — muesli absorbs curd in 2 minutes |
| Step 4 | Add sliced banana (வாழைப்பழம்) + papaya (பப்பாளி) or any fruit |
| Step 5 | Sprinkle 1 tsp chia seeds (சியா விதை) or pumpkin seeds (பூசணி விதை) |
| Step 6 | Optional: 1 tsp honey |

---

### 10. Whole Wheat Bread + Peanut Butter Plate (ரொட்டி + வெண்ணெய் தட்டு)
**When to prepare:** 1 minute before dinner
**No prep at all**

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| Step 1 | Take 2 slices whole wheat bread (கோதுமை ரொட்டி) |
| Step 2 | Spread 1.5–2 tablespoons natural peanut butter (நிலக்கடலை வெண்ணெய்) on each slice |
| Step 3 | Place sliced banana (வாழைப்பழம்) or apple (ஆப்பிள்) on top |
| Step 4 | Sprinkle 1 tsp flax seeds (ஆளி விதை) or sesame seeds (எள்) |
| Step 5 | Eat with a small bowl of curd (தயிர்) or a glass of buttermilk (மோர்) |

> **Check peanut butter label:** It should say only "peanuts + salt" — no palm oil, no hydrogenated fat, no added sugar. Brands: Sundrop Natural, Pintola, etc.

## PART 5 — Quick Daily Checklist (Print or Screenshot This)

### Every Morning (6:00–6:20 AM)
- [ ] Drink warm water (சூடான நீர்) + lemon (எலுமிச்சை) at 6:10 AM
- [ ] Peel soaked almonds (பாதாம்) from last night
- [ ] Check overnight oats / chia pudding — is it thick and ready?
- [ ] Check sprouts — rinse, drain, cover again
- [ ] Buy fresh curd (தயிர்) from local dairy (or ask someone to bring it)

### Every Night (9:00–9:30 PM) — Takes Only 5 Minutes
- [ ] Check daily plan for tomorrow — what needs soaking?
- [ ] Soak oats OR chia seeds (as per tomorrow's breakfast)
- [ ] Soak 10 almonds in small bowl of water (பாதாம்)
- [ ] Rinse and re-cover sprouts if it is sprouting days (Tue-Wed, Fri-Sat)
- [ ] Hang curd if tomorrow's lunch needs hung curd dressing

### Two Times Per Week (Sunday 9 PM + Wednesday 9 PM)
- [ ] Soak 1 cup moong dal (பாசிப் பருப்பு) in 3 cups water
- [ ] Soak 1/2 cup chana (கொண்டைக்கடலை) in 2 cups water
- [ ] Next morning: drain both, tie in wet cloth → start sprouting

## PART 6 — Troubleshooting

| Problem | What Happened | Solution |
|---|---|---|
| Oats are too thick / paste-like | Too much soaking time or too little water | Add a splash of water or milk, stir well |
| Oats are too watery | Too much water added | Add more dry oats or chia seeds to thicken |
| Chia pudding didn't set | Too little chia or too much milk | Next time use 2 tbsp chia per 200ml milk; stir 3 times in first 10 min |
| Poha is too mushy | Soaked too long or too thin variety | Use thick poha (கொழுத்த அவல்); soak max 8–10 minutes |
| Sprouts smell sour / bad | Left too long without rinsing or hot weather | Discard. Rinse sprouts every 8 hours in hot weather; use within 1 day |
| Sprouts are not sprouting | Water too warm or not drained properly | Rinse in cool water; ensure cloth is damp not soaking wet |
| Bread got hard / stale | Left open in humid air | Wrap in paper or cloth; eat within 2 days of opening |
| Hung curd is too thin | Cloth holes too big or not enough time | Use thicker muslin cloth; hang for at least 8–10 hours |
| Peanut butter oil separated | Natural separation — normal | Stir with a spoon until combined; store jar upside down |
